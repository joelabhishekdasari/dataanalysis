Chapter 1 — The Problem

How serious is the late-return problem across BorrowBox?

We start by establishing the scale of the problem across all five Key Performance Indicators. Before the solution was implemented, 23% of all equipment loans were returned late, cascading into disruptions, delays, and staff overrides. The five KPIs below are the measurable targets this project was designed to improve.

Chapter 4 — What Is the Real Cost?

KPI 2 (RDR) in depth — when late returns hurt the next user

The Reservation Disruption Rate (RDR = Σ disruption / Σ is_late × 100) measures how frequently a late return causes a service delivery failure for another user. A disruption means a borrower arrived at a hub to pick up their reserved item and found it not yet returned. This chapter quantifies that downstream cost.

Chapter 5 — Is the Current Intervention Working?

KPI 4 (ARDR) and KPI 5 (SOR) — Why reminders fail and what replaced them

Before the prototype, the only intervention was an automated reminder sent before the due date. The data shows this had no measurable effect on LRR (KPI 1). ARDR (KPI 4) was 0% — nothing was being flagged proactively. SOR (KPI 5) was high at 8.9% because staff were constantly reacting manually. This tab shows the evidence behind replacing reminders with the At-Risk Monitor.

The prototype replaces this with the At-Risk Monitor — a risk-scoring system that uses Category Weight + Hub Weight + Days Remaining + User Segment to flag items proactively before the due date passes, raising ARDR from 0% to 64.9% and lowering SOR from 8.9% to 2.9%.
